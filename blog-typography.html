Cover typography

Some recent favorites

I am quite passionate about typography and I collect book covers that do something interesting with it. Among my recent favorites are these covers, which treat the text conceptually — the typography plays with the meaning of the represented words.

I also came across a couple of covers where the typography, so to speak, exceeded to covers themselves.

I found some interesting covers where a certain emotional impression was achieved by applying a visual effect to the text itself.

And a whole bunch of covers where the text was made into part of the image or where it is in some sort of dialogue with it.

And finally, a rather curious example of a book cover with the typography… missing.

This is an interesting development in book cover design in a world where books are predominantly sold online rather than in brick-and-mortar shops. Notice that on Amazon, for example, you will always see the title of the book as well as the author’s name and some description right next to the image of the cover.

Some authors, in this case Hugh Howey, reason that therefore there’s no need to include such details on the cover itself. The cover can be an unobstructed artwork.

I fully agree with the reasoning and yet, I have to say, I prefer covers with the text on them. There is just something about the confluence of words and images that I find magincal.
